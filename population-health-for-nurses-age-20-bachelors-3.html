Population Health for Nurses (Age 20, US Bachelor's 3)

Apply public health principles and health equity frameworks to nursing practice across communities.

Subject: Nursing
Level: Bachelor's
Age / Grade: Age 20 / US Bachelor's 3
Language: English
Formats: PDF
Download English PDF

About This Textbook

The text grounds nurses in social determinants, policy, and epidemiology while integrating the nursing process into community partnerships. Case studies emphasize culturally responsive care and interprofessional collaboration.

Available Formats

  • English PDF: Download

Table of Contents

  • Preserving the Health of Populations and Communities (Foundations of Population Health; Public/Community Health in Practice; Population Health Outcomes)
  • Issues and Challenges of Population Health (Demographic Trends; Structural Racism and Systemic Inequities; Policy and Regulatory Conditions; Social Determinants and Health Disparities)
  • Population-Based Practice and the Tenets of Public Health (Socio-Ecological Perspectives; Evidence-Based Decision-Making; Epidemiology; Pandemics and Infectious Disease; Environmental Health; Health Promotion and Disease Prevention)
  • Merging Public Health Principles with the Nursing Process (Community Assessment and Diagnosis; Planning Health Promotion and Education; Implementation and Evaluation Considerations)
  • Culturally Congruent Care (Cultural Influences; Transcultural Nursing; Culturally and Linguistically Responsive Care; Designing Inclusive Programs; Managing the Dynamics of Difference)
  • Caring for Populations and Communities (Across the Lifespan; Vulnerable Populations; Families; Practice Settings; Care Transitions; Crisis Response; Disaster Management)
  • The Nurse’s Role as Advocate and Leader (Advocacy; Policy Engagement; Leading Population Health Improvement)

Teaching & Learning Tips

Assign windshield surveys or community asset maps so learners observe determinants firsthand. Debrief policy case studies to connect advocacy strategies with frontline nursing experiences.
