Writing Guide with Handbook (Age 18, US Bachelor's 1)

Connect everyday rhetoric with academic and professional writing for diverse audiences.

About This Textbook

The guide bridges personal expression and academic discourse with inclusive examples, genre-based chapters, and a robust handbook section. Students explore multimodal communication while learning processes for research, revision, and reflection.

Available Formats

  • English PDF: Download

Table of Contents

  • The Things We Carry: Experience, Culture, and Language (The Digital World: Building on What You Already Know to Respond Critically; Language, Identity, and Culture: Exploring, Employing, Embracing; Literacy Narrative: Building Bridges, Bridging Gaps)
  • Bridging the Divide Between Personal Identity and Academia (Memoir or Personal Narrative; Profile; Proposal; Evaluation or Review; Analytical Report; Rhetorical Analysis; Position Argument; Reasoning Strategies; Argumentative Research; Research Process; Annotated Bibliography; Case Study Profile)
  • Navigating Rhetoric in Real Life (Print or Textual Analysis; Image Analysis; Multimodal and Online Writing; Scripting for the Public Forum; Portfolio Reflection)

Teaching & Learning Tips

Use reflective journals to help students connect identity-focused chapters with academic writing goals. Peer workshops that emphasize audience awareness encourage experimentation with modality, tone, and structure.
