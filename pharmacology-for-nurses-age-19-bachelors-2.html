Pharmacology for Nurses (Age 19, US Bachelor's 2)

Strengthen medication safety with systems-focused pharmacology aligned to nursing practice.

About This Textbook

Designed for introductory nursing pharmacology courses, the text blends mechanism explanations with patient-focused nursing considerations. Case scenarios and NCLEX-style questions reinforce safe administration, monitoring, and education.

Available Formats

  • English PDF: Download

Table of Contents

  • Introduction to Pharmacology for Nurses (Introduction to Pharmacology; Drug Administration; Ethics, Legal Considerations, and Safety)
  • Homeostasis (Fluids and Electrolytes; Vitamins, Minerals, and Complementary Therapies)
  • Immune System (Inflammatory Response; Anti-infective Drugs; Cancer Therapy and Pharmacology)
  • Nervous System and Drugs for Mental Well-Being (Neurologic Foundations; Neuromuscular Disorders; Psychopharmacologic Agents; Pain Response and Substance Use Disorder Treatments)
  • Cardiovascular System (Cardiac Physiology; Antidysrhythmic, Antihypertensive, Antianginal, and Heart Failure Drugs; Anticoagulant and Lipid-Lowering Agents; Emergency and Shock Drugs)
  • Respiratory System (Upper and Lower Respiratory Disorder Drugs)
  • Endocrine System (Hypothalamic, Pituitary, and Adrenal Drugs; Thyroid and Parathyroid Agents; Diabetic Pharmacology)
  • Digestive System (Gastrointestinal Agents; Hyperacidity and Antiulcer Drugs; Weight Management Pharmacotherapy)
  • Renal and Urinary Systems (Diuretics; Urinary and Bladder Disorder Drugs)
  • Reproductive System (Reproductive Health Pharmacology; Transgender and Nonbinary Care)
  • Sensory and Dermatologic Systems (Ophthalmic, Otic, and Dermatologic Drugs)

Teaching & Learning Tips

Use medication cards or digital flash decks to connect drug classes with nursing implications. Encourage students to rehearse patient education dialogues to strengthen communication about side effects and adherence.
