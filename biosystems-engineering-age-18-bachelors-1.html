Introduce students to engineering principles that optimize biological, agricultural, and environmental systems.

About This Textbook

The text provides a broad overview of the systems approach engineers use to solve biological and agricultural problems. Students explore instrumentation, machinery, resource management, and process control through examples that highlight sustainability and innovation.

Available Formats

  • PDF: Ready for lecture slides, lab manuals, or offline study.

  • Machinery Systems
  • Natural Resources and Environmental Systems
  • Plant, Animal, and Facility Systems
  • Processing Systems

Teaching & Learning Tips

Pair chapters with design challenges rooted in local agricultural or environmental contexts. Site visits and guest speakers from industry can demonstrate how biosystems engineers apply classroom knowledge.
