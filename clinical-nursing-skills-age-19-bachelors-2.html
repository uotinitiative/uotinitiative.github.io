Clinical Nursing Skills (Age 19, US Bachelor's 2)

Build clinical judgment with procedure guides that span assessment, intervention, and evaluation.

About This Textbook

The text pairs evidence-based procedures with the clinical judgment steps required to deliver comprehensive care. Learners practice communication, assessment, medication administration, and documentation through scenarios that reflect diverse patient populations and care settings.

Available Formats

  • English PDF: Download

Table of Contents

  • Concepts of Comprehensive Clinical Care (Role of the Nurse; Evolution of the Nurse's Role; Patient Communication and Interviewing; Obtaining a Complete Health History; Cultural Competence and Assessment; Infection Prevention Techniques)
  • General Assessment and Special Considerations (Hygiene; Wound and Burn Assessment and Care; Activity Assessment and Management; Specimen Collection and Laboratory Testing)
  • Medication Management (Principles of Medication Administration; Medication Administration Procedures; Intravenous Administration; Miscellaneous Medication Administration)
  • Health Assessment and Promotion (General Survey, Anthropometric Measurement, and Vital Signs; Pain Assessment; Nutrition Assessment; Oxygenation and Perfusion; Fluids, Electrolytes, and Elimination; Psychosocial Assessment)
  • Head-to-Toe Systems Assessment (Integumentary; Head and Neck; Thorax, Lungs, Breast, and Lymphatic System; Cardiovascular and Peripheral Vascular System; Musculoskeletal System; Neurological System; Abdomen)
  • Clinical Judgment and Critical Thinking

Teaching & Learning Tips

Use simulation labs or virtual scenarios to let students rehearse sequences from assessment through documentation. Ask learners to craft SBAR handoffs after each skill check to reinforce communication precision.
