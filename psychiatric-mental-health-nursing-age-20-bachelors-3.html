Develop therapeutic communication and evidence-based interventions for mental health care.

About This Textbook

Designed for a single-semester course, the book aligns DSM-5 disorders with therapeutic nursing responses. It emphasizes trauma-informed care, interprofessional collaboration, and advocacy for clients across the lifespan.

Available Formats

  • English PDF: Download

Table of Contents

  • Theories, Practice, and Trends (Foundations of Psychiatric-Mental Health Nursing; Theories and Therapies; Communication, Perception, and Assessment; Neurobiology and Pharmacological Standards)
  • Therapeutic Considerations in Mental Health (Therapeutic Settings; Therapeutic Relationships; Interprofessional Care; Cultural Considerations; Social and Emotional Concerns)
  • Client Rights and Legal Issues (Legal and Ethical Guidelines; Court Involvement; Self-Determination and Advocacy)
  • Psychiatric Nursing Practice (Clinical Guidelines; Neurocognitive Disorders; Schizophrenia Spectrum and Psychotic Disorders; Mood Disorders and Suicide; Anxiety, OCD, and Trauma-Stressor Disorders; Personality Disorders; Substance Use and Misuse; Eating Disorders; Somatic Symptom Disorders; Sexual Dysfunction and Gender Dysphoria)
  • Psychiatric Nursing Practice for Special Populations (Children and Adolescents; Older Adults)
  • Mental Health Care in the Community (Community Treatment; Adjuncts to Treatments; Current Trends and Growing Needs; Critical Thinking in Psychiatric-Mental Health Nursing)

Teaching & Learning Tips

Incorporate standardized patient interviews to practice therapeutic communication and boundary setting. Encourage reflective debriefs after simulations to build resilience and self-awareness.
