Anatomy and Physiology (Age 18, US Bachelor’s 1)

Explore the structure and function of the human body with a comprehensive, systems-based text for introductory undergraduate courses.

Level: Bachelor’s
Age / Grade: Age 18 / US Bachelor’s 1
Language: English
Formats: PDF
Download PDF

About This Textbook

The text supports two-semester anatomy and physiology sequences with clear explanations, system-focused organization, and clinically relevant examples. Detailed illustrations and review activities reinforce mastery for students in life sciences, nursing, and allied health programs.

Available Formats

  • PDF: Download for lecture preparation, lab integration, and offline reference.

Table of Contents

  • Levels of Organization (Introduction to the Human Body, Chemical Level, Cellular Level, Tissue Level)
  • Support and Movement (Integumentary System, Bone Tissue and the Skeletal System, Axial Skeleton, Appendicular Skeleton, Joints, Muscle Tissue, Muscular System)
  • Regulation, Integration, and Control (Nervous System and Nervous Tissue, Anatomy of the Nervous System, Somatic Nervous System, Autonomic Nervous System, Neurological Exam, Endocrine System)
  • Fluids and Transport (Cardiovascular System: Blood, The Heart, Blood Vessels and Circulation, Lymphatic and Immune System)
  • Energy, Maintenance, and Environmental Exchange (Respiratory System, Digestive System, Metabolism and Nutrition, Urinary System, Fluid, Electrolyte, and Acid-Base Balance)
  • Human Development and Continuity of Life (Reproductive System, Development and Inheritance)

Teaching & Learning Tips

Pair readings with lab dissections or virtual simulations to connect structure and function. Encourage students to create concept maps for each system to visualize interdependencies across the body.
