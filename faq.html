Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is the mission of the Universal Open Textbook Initiative (UOTI)?

Our mission is to become the only place in the world that people need to visit in order to obtain openly licensed, high-quality textbooks in a language they understand. We seek to provide a foundation of educational content for everyone across the world to use and build on.

2. How do you achieve this mission?

First, by providing open textbooks that incorporate the practices found in the best performing countries. Then, by ensuring the same material is available in different languages.

3. How do I find the content I am looking for?

On the home page, select your language, learner age, and subject from the available options to get the download link. You can also use our Master Curriculum Document to find information on the components covered in a textbook obtained through a particular selection.

4. How do I learn about the specific terms of use for a particular textbook? Can I use these textbooks for commercial purposes?

The specific terms of use are provided in each textbook’s copyright page. With some exceptions, commerical usage is permitted.

5. Who created these textbooks?

All the textbooks are created and reviewed by educators and subject matter experts. You can find the credits in each textbook’s copyright page.

6. How can I help?

We welcome contributions from philanthropists, educators, subject matter experts, translators, and others. Please contact us via email at contact@uotinitiative.org.
