Maternal-Newborn Nursing (Age 20, US Bachelor's 3)

Support inclusive, evidence-based care from preconception through postpartum and newborn transitions.

About This Textbook

The text integrates physiology, cultural humility, and the Clinical Judgment Measurement Model to prepare nurses for perinatal care. Scenario-based learning encourages early recognition of complications and collaborative response planning.

Table of Contents

  • Introduction to Maternal-Newborn and Women's Health Nursing (Foundations; Culturally Competent Nursing Care)
  • Women's Health Through the Lifespan (Health Promotion; Influences on Fertility; Family Planning; Reproductive System Disorders; Breast Disorders; Violence Against Women)
  • Care During Pregnancy (Pregnancy; Prenatal Care; Pregnancy at Risk; Prenatal Testing; Childbirth Education Options)
  • Care During Labor and Birth (Process of Labor and Birth; Fetal and Uterine Monitoring; Pain Management; Nursing Care and Interventions; Complications of Labor and Birth)
  • Care During the Postpartum Period (Postpartum Care; Postpartum Complications)
  • Newborn Care (Immediate Care; Newborn Assessment; Care of the Typical Newborn; Care of the Newborn at Risk; Perinatal Bereavement; Unfolding Case Study)

Teaching & Learning Tips

Incorporate fetal heart tracing drills and debriefs to sharpen rapid assessment skills. Encourage reflective journaling on cultural humility scenarios to deepen awareness of bias and communication needs.
